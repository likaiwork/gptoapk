# What is Facebook? Complete 2026 Guide for Beginners

## What is Facebook?

**Facebook** is the world's largest social networking platform, founded by Mark Zuckerberg in 2004 at Harvard University. With over 3 billion monthly active users as of 2026, it's not just a website — it's the digital town square where people connect, share, and discover.

In simple terms, Facebook lets you:

- **Connect** with friends and family
- **Share** photos, videos, and life updates
- **Discover** news, entertainment, and communities
- **Buy and sell** through Marketplace
- **Promote** businesses and brands

The name "Facebook" comes from the traditional "face book" — a printed directory of student photos and names given to new college students. Mark Zuckerberg digitized this concept and created a global phenomenon.

## What is Facebook Used For?

### 1. Staying Connected

The core purpose of Facebook is connecting people. You can:

- **Add friends** — Search by name, email, or phone
- **Follow pages** — Celebrities, brands, news outlets
- **Join groups** — Communities around any topic imaginable
- **Message friends** — Through Facebook Messenger

### 2. Sharing Content

**Timeline / Profile:**
Each user has a personal timeline where they post:
- Status updates (text)
- Photos and albums
- Videos (short-form Reels or long-form Watch)
- Links and articles
- Live streams

**News Feed:**
The algorithm-curated homepage shows content from friends, pages, and groups you follow — plus recommended content and ads.

### 3. Marketplace

Facebook's built-in classifieds platform (similar to Craigslist or eBay) lets you:
- List items for sale
- Browse items nearby
- Message sellers directly
- Categorize by price, location, and category

### 4. Groups

Facebook Groups are the platform's most powerful community feature:

- **Public groups** — Anyone can join and see posts
- **Closed groups** — Anyone can find, but must request to join
- **Secret groups** — Invite-only, invisible in search

### 5. Watch and Reels

- **Watch** — Long-form video content (competes with YouTube)
- **Reels** — Short-form video (competes with TikTok)
- **Live** — Real-time streaming with audience interaction

## How to Register on Facebook

### What You Need

- **Email address or phone number** — Gmail or Outlook recommended
- **Your real name** — Facebook enforces real-name policy
- **Date of birth** — You must be at least 13 years old
- **Internet connection**

### Step-by-Step Registration

**Step 1:** Go to **facebook.com** or open the Facebook app
**Step 2:** Click **"Create new account"**
**Step 3:** Enter your:
- First and last name
- Email or mobile number
- Password
- Date of birth
- Gender

**Step 4:** Click **"Sign Up"**
**Step 5:** Check your email or phone for a verification code
**Step 6:** Enter the code to verify your account
**Step 7:** Add a profile photo (optional but recommended)
**Step 8:** Find friends (or skip)

> **Pro tip:** Use a real name and real photo. Fake accounts are quickly detected and banned.

## Facebook Login Guide

### How to Log In

- **Website:** facebook.com → enter email/phone + password
- **App:** Open the app → enter credentials → tap Log In

### Login URL

Direct link: **facebook.com/login**

### Forgot Your Password?

1. Click **"Forgotten password?"**
2. Enter your email or phone number
3. Receive verification code
4. Reset your password

### Account Recovery

If you lose access:
1. Go to facebook.com/login/identify
2. Enter your name, email, or phone
3. Verify identity through trusted contacts or email
4. Follow recovery steps

**Prepare in advance:**
- ✅ Set up 3-5 trusted contacts
- ✅ Keep recovery email up to date
- ✅ Enable two-factor authentication
- ✅ Save backup codes

## Facebook Founder: Mark Zuckerberg

### Biography

- **Full name:** Mark Zuckerberg
- **Born:** May 14, 1984, White Plains, New York
- **Education:** Harvard University (dropped out)
- **Spouse:** Priscilla Chan (married 2012)
- **Net worth (2026):** ~$200 billion

### The Facebook Story

Zuckerberg launched Facebook from his Harvard dorm room on February 4, 2004. Originally called "TheFacebook," it expanded to other Ivy League schools, then universities across the US, and eventually the entire world.

**Key milestones:**
- **2004:** Facebook launched at Harvard
- **2006:** Opened to anyone over 13
- **2012:** IPO at $38/share (valued at $104 billion)
- **2012:** Acquired Instagram for $1 billion
- **2014:** Acquired WhatsApp for $19 billion
- **2021:** Renamed parent company to Meta
- **2026:** Facebook remains the world's #1 social network

### Controversies

Zuckerberg has faced significant criticism over:
- Cambridge Analytica data scandal (2018)
- Spread of misinformation and fake news
- Antitrust concerns and regulation
- Mental health impact on teens
- Content moderation decisions

## The Facebook to Meta Rebrand

On October 28, 2021, Facebook announced its parent company would be renamed **Meta**.

### Why the Rebrand?

1. **Strategic shift toward the metaverse** — The next evolution of the internet
2. **Brand distancing** — Separate the company from Facebook's privacy scandals
3. **Product unification** — Connect Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp, and VR products under one umbrella

### What Changed?

| Before | After |
|--------|-------|
| Company: Facebook, Inc. | Company: Meta Platforms, Inc. |
| Stock: FB | Stock: META |
| Tagline: "Bringing the world closer together" | Mission: "Help bring the metaverse to life" |
| VR: Oculus | VR: Meta Quest (rebranded) |
| Products: Separate brands | Products: Under Meta umbrella |

### What Stayed the Same?

- Facebook app is still called **Facebook**
- Instagram, WhatsApp, Messenger keep their names
- You still log into "Facebook" as always

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is Facebook and why is it called that?

Facebook is a social networking platform. The name comes from "face book," a student directory (with photos) that US colleges traditionally give to incoming freshmen.

### Is Facebook free?

Yes, Facebook is completely free to use. Meta generates revenue through targeted advertising.

### Can I use Facebook without a profile picture?

Yes, but accounts with profile photos are more trusted and less likely to be flagged as suspicious.

### What's the difference between Facebook and Meta?

**Meta** is the parent company (like Alphabet for Google). **Facebook** is one of Meta's products, alongside Instagram, WhatsApp, and Messenger.

### Why can't I log into Facebook?

Common reasons:
- Wrong password (use "Forgot password" to reset)
- Account temporarily locked (unusual login detected)
- No internet connection
- Server issues (check downdetector.com)

### How many people use Facebook?

Over 3 billion monthly active users as of 2026 — roughly 40% of the world's population.

### How do I delete my Facebook account?

Settings → Your Facebook Information → Deactivation and Deletion → Delete Account. Note: deletion is permanent after 30 days.

### What happens to Messenger if I delete Facebook?

You lose access to Messenger as well, since it requires a Facebook account.
